Research Focus

Our multidisciplinary approach spans cancer biology, translational medicine, and diagnostic technology.

On this page
Transcription Factors Biomarker Discovery IVD & Biosensors

1. Transcription Factors in Cancer

Transcription factors are critical regulators of gene expression often dysregulated in cancer. Our lab focuses on targeting "undruggable" targets such as NRF2, CLOCK, and FOSB.

We employ a dual strategy using both small molecule inhibitors and aptamers (nucleic acid-based ligands) to disrupt the protein-DNA or protein-protein interactions of these factors. By modulating their activity, we aim to overcome chemotherapy resistance and improve therapeutic outcomes in solid tumors.

2. Diagnostic & Prognostic Biomarker Discovery

Bridging the gap between basic science and the clinic, this research axis is closely integrated with our Medical Oncology Ph.D. program. We utilize high-throughput sequencing and proteomic approaches to identify novel biomarkers.

Our work involves analyzing both preclinical models and clinical patient samples (tissue and liquid biopsy). The goal is to discover robust signatures that can predict patient response to therapy or signal early disease recurrence.

3. In Vitro Diagnostics (IVD) & Biosensors

Translating our findings into practical tools is a key priority. Through our spin-off industrial enterprise, we are developing advanced biosensing platforms.

These efforts are focused on creating rapid, sensitive assays for drug resistance. By detecting specific resistance markers directly from patient samples, we aim to facilitate personalized treatment decisions and reduce the time to effective therapy.
